Competitor Pricing

Competitor pricing served as the foundation for VitaNexus's pricing strategy, ensuring strong market positioning while maintaining affordability for users. However, due to the integration of proprietary AI systems and premium data analytics, prices are set slightly above average to sustain operational costs and ensure long-term profitability.

Initial Revenue Estimations

VitaNexus's financial projections are grounded in realistic adoption rates within Australia's growing digital fitness market. Approximately 39% (≈10.6 million) of Australian adults use fitness or health apps (Australian Institute of Fitness, 2024). Based on national download behaviour, an estimated average of 4% of existing fitness-app users are likely to download a new app annually (42Matters, 2024), resulting in ≈ 424,000 VitaNexus downloads during the first year.

Although 71% of fitness-app users quit by the third month due to limitations (as discussed in The Market section), VitaNexus aims to address these issues through AI-driven personalisation and holistic tracking. Consequently, only 30% of users are expected to churn within the first year, maintaining an estimated ≈ 297,000 active users.

Break-Even Analysis

Initial Year Calculations

VitaNexus will reach break-even when approximately 943 premium users remain active, generating a total of $160,310 AUD, which covers all projected annual operating costs of $160,200. Given the projected 252,450 active users, only 0.37% of users need to convert to premium for VitaNexus to become profitable, a highly achievable target for a first-year digital fitness business.
